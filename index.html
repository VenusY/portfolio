About Me

My journey began in secondary school when I chose computing for my GCSEs. While learning Python, I discovered that I had a passion for programming and building projects. I loved the idea that you could build anything you wanted as long as you had perseverence and an imagination— it was a thought that was simultaneously intriguing and intimidating.

Unfortunately, I never followed through with this interest as I was still convinced that I wanted to go into law during this period of my life.

I would later rediscover my passion for programming when I relearned the basics of Python in preparation for a law apprenticeship. I found out that programming could be useful for automating large-scale, repetitive tasks in law, and this led me on a research journey until I finally landed on web development.

Since then, I've been building projects and learning as much as I can about web development and programming in general. My main focus these days is familiarising myself with tools like NPM and Webpack, as well as GitHub to prepare myself for an official entry into the industry.

When I'm not staring at documentation or at war with CSS, you can find me buried in a book or zipping around with friends on Hoxxes IV, trying not to die to a glyphid.
